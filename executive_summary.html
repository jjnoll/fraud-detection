Fraud Detection System Correction

Our automated fraud scoring tool contained four logic errors that caused it to rate the riskiest transactions as the safest ones. All errors have been identified, corrected, and validated.

What Was Wrong — and What Was Fixed

The system evaluates each transaction against six risk signals. Four of those signals were inverted: the higher the danger, the lower the score the transaction received. Each fix simply reverses that direction.

📱

BEFORE — High-risk device lowered the score

When a transaction came from a device flagged as high risk (common in fraud attacks), the system awarded it a 25-point safety bonus.

📱

AFTER — High-risk device raises the score

A high-risk device now correctly adds 25 points, making the transaction more likely to be reviewed.

🌍

BEFORE — International transactions lowered the score

Transactions from overseas — a well-known fraud indicator — were treated as a sign of trustworthiness, reducing the score by 15 points.

🌍

AFTER — International transactions raise the score

Cross-border transactions now correctly add 15 points to the risk score.

⚡

BEFORE — Rapid spending lowered the score

When an account made 6 or more transactions in 24 hours — a classic fraud pattern — the system treated this as low risk and subtracted 20 points.

⚡

AFTER — Rapid spending raises the score

High transaction velocity now correctly adds 20 points, triggering review of rapid-fire spending patterns.

🔁

BEFORE — Repeat offenders lowered the score

Customers with a history of chargebacks — the strongest predictor of future fraud — were considered safer than new customers.

🔁

AFTER — Chargeback history raises the score

Prior chargebacks now correctly increase the risk score, with repeat offenders receiving the highest uplift.

What This Means Going Forward

  • Transactions with the highest combination of fraud signals — risky device, overseas origin, rapid spending, and a chargeback history — will now receive the maximum risk score and be prioritised for review.
  • The fraud operations team will no longer be working from a list that buries the most dangerous transactions at the bottom.
  • The correction does not affect legitimate low-risk customers. Domestic transactions from clean accounts with normal spending patterns continue to score low.
  • Automated tests have been added to prevent these specific errors from being re-introduced in the future.
